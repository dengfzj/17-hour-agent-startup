# Research and Selection

Recorded on: 2026-06-11

## Research Method

This project used online research and multiple sub-agents in parallel:

- Commercial research: market demand, willingness to pay, and alternatives.
- Product evaluation: whether a sellable product could be built quickly.
- Engineering evaluation: whether a mature local product loop could be completed.
- Risk review: compliance, security, payment evidence, and automation boundaries.
- Revenue operations review: whether the system could support real paid concierge pilots.

## Selection Criteria

Candidate directions were scored by:

- Market pain intensity.
- Willingness to pay.
- AI automation value.
- Path from MVP to mature product.
- Compliance risk.
- Customer acquisition difficulty.
- Ability to collect paid-pilot revenue first.

## Final Choices

### BidFlow Local

Why selected:

- Directly maps to the revenue funnel.
- Local service businesses often have high job values.
- Missed quote follow-up is easy for customers to understand.
- AI is useful for scoring, drafting, reminders, and evidence organization.

### ReputeLoop

Why selected:

- Local reviews directly affect trust and conversion.
- Many businesses lack compliant responses and customer recovery workflows.
- AI is useful for risk triage, reply drafts, and recovery plans.
- It can become a recurring subscription product.

## Rejected or Deferred Directions

- Generic content generation: crowded market and weak willingness to pay.
- Generic CRM: too broad and insufficiently differentiated.
- Fully autonomous sales agent: high compliance and real-world risk.
- Fake review or review-suppression tooling: violates platform and regulatory boundaries.

## Conclusion

The best current route is to sell BidFlow first for higher-ticket revenue recovery, then use ReputeLoop as a lower-price recurring subscription. Both products share the same sales, payment, onboarding, delivery, and evidence foundation.
